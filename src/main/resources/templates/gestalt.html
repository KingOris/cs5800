Gestalt
Composition #1 - Figure/Ground relationship - I arranged the 9 squares to form a figure which stands out from the white background which is functioning as the ground. This high relationship between my ground and the figure I created made a strong design. Even though the whole composition is in 2D. The figure I created seems to be in front of the ground.
Composition #2 - Symmetry - I used 9 squares to form a large arrow, which is symmetrical from left to the right. I adjusted all the rotation angles for all the 9 squares to make sure this arrow is perfectly symmetrical.
Composition #3 - Proximity - I put all 9 squares in places relatively very close to each other in order to create a grouping image since they all look very similar. This way, my eyes would recognize these 9 squares as a single group and accept them as a single object.
Composition #4 - Closure - The goal is to create a shape that can be seen even though the shape is not fully completed or closed. I used the squares to form the outline of a triangle. I can see the triangle clearly even though the triangle is not completely enclosed.
Composition #5 - Continuation - The goal is to create a composition where the eye is carried smoothly into a line or a curve without any disturbance. I arrange the squares for they look like they are all following a curve.
