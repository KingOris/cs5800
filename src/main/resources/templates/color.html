Color Theory

For the first color assignment, I made a Color Wheel with all 12 colors on the color wheel I bought. The first inner circle is formed by the tint colors of these associated 12 colors. And the inner-most circle is formed by the shades of these 12 colors. I googled all the colors’ RGB values and entered these values inside Adobe Illustrator to create color wheel with very accurate colors. For the first image, which is Achromatic. I used 9 different values between black and white to create layers and form a tunnel-like visual effect. For basic primary colors. I chose blue as the background and used 7 different shapes filled with either yellow or red on top of the blue background. This way I can see the contrasting effects between blue and red and yellow. For the Complementary colors image, I chose blue and orange as the complementary color pair and used the tints and shades of both blue and orange to create a composition that contains motion. I used a lot of circles and ellipses in different sizes and created a circular motion in the composition. For Analogous Colors with a warm relationship I used yellow, orange and yellow-orange since they are adjacent to each other, they don’t contain all three primary colors and they are all warm colors. For Analogous Colors with a cool relationship, I used green, blue and blue-green for the same reasons.
